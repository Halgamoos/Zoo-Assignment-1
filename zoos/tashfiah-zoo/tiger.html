Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger native to the South Asian region are fierce and quite solitary creatures. Notable for their stripes they are unfortunatley only 2,500 left in the wild. They are also known to be highly accurate in killing their prey as they are carnivores. They are known to be the biggest wild cat. Though there is a small number there are bengal tigers with white fur due to a recessive gene and these tigers are mostly found in Assam, India. Unfortunatley, poaching is a popular practice with Bengal Tigers but in many countries across South Asia have been stated as illegal to protect their population and prevent extinction.
